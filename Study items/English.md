## 1. **Clarity & Grammar (Writing Drills)**

- **Daily rewrite exercise**: Take one of your own sentences from chat or email and rewrite it in 2–3 clearer ways.  
    Example:
    
    - Original: _“do you i need to develop me in some skils”_
        
    - Rewrite 1: _“Do I need to develop some skills?”_
        
    - Rewrite 2: _“What skills should I focus on developing?”_
        
- **Tool support**: Use Grammarly or Hemingway Editor to catch errors and shorten sentences.
    

---

## 2. **Confidence & Flow (Speaking Practice)**

- **One-minute story**: Every day, record yourself speaking for 1 minute about _any_ topic (your day, a hobby, an opinion). Listen back and note filler words (“um, like, you know”).
    
- **Mirror technique**: Practice speaking in front of a mirror. This builds eye contact and body language awareness.
    
- **Conversation challenge**: Ask one open-ended question in your daily life (e.g., to a colleague, friend, or even online). Practice listening actively and responding with follow-up questions.
    

---

## 3. **Professional Style (Structured Communication)**

- **Frameworks to practice**:
    
    - **PREP**: _Point → Reason → Example → Point again_
        
    - **STAR**: _Situation → Task → Action → Result_
        
    - Try writing 2–3 LinkedIn-style posts or short essays using these frameworks.
        
- **Summarization exercise**: Take a long article/video and write a **3-sentence summary**. This trains you to be concise and clear.
    

---

## Bonus Habits 🌟

- Read 15 minutes daily (blogs, articles, or books) → copy the style you like.
    
- Expand vocabulary: learn 1–2 new words a day and use them in conversation.
    
- Seek feedback: ask a colleague or friend how clear your message was after explaining something.
